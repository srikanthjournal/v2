Deep Learning-Based Intrusion Detection System for IoT Networks: A Comparative Study of CNN and LSTM Architectures
Priya Sharma, Rajesh Kumar Gupta, Ananya Singh — Dept. of Computer Science, IIT Delhi, India
This paper presents a comparative analysis of CNN and LSTM architectures for intrusion detection in IoT environments. Using the UNSW-NB15 and TON-IoT datasets, our proposed hybrid model achieves 98.7% detection accuracy, outperforming state-of-the-art approaches while maintaining low computational overhead suitable for resource-constrained IoT devices.
Keywords:IoT SecurityDeep LearningCNNLSTMIntrusion Detection

Federated Learning in Healthcare: Privacy-Preserving Collaborative Model Training — A Systematic Review
Mohammed Al-Hassan, Fatima Zahra, Chen Wei — King Abdullah University of Science & Technology, Saudi Arabia; Peking University, China
This systematic review examines 94 peer-reviewed studies on federated learning in healthcare (2018–2024), identifying key challenges including communication overhead, non-IID data distributions, Byzantine fault tolerance, and regulatory compliance. A unified taxonomy and future research directions are proposed.
Keywords:Federated LearningHealthcare AIPrivacyMedical Imaging

Smart Traffic Management Using Reinforcement Learning and Edge Computing: Bengaluru Metropolitan Case Study
Karthik Rajan, Divya Mohan, Arjun Pillai — Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, India
A RL-based adaptive signal control system deployed at 48 intersections in Bengaluru reduced average vehicle wait times by 34% and emergency vehicle response times by 52% over a 6-month evaluation using Q-learning augmented with real-time edge-computed inputs.
