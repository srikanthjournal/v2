Peer Review Process

IJITRA uses a rigorous double-blind peer review system ensuring fairness, quality, and scientific integrity in every publication decision.

Average Timeline at a Glance

3–5Desk Review (days)
2–3Reviewer Assignment (days)
10–15Peer Review (days)
1–2Decision Issued (days)
~18Total Average (days)
ℹ️ All manuscripts undergo double-blind peer review — both authors and reviewers are anonymous to each other. Editorial decisions are made solely on scientific merit. IJITRA follows COPE guidelines throughout the process.

Step-by-Step Review Workflow

1
📨 Manuscript Submission
Day 0
Author submits the manuscript through the online submission system with cover letter, author information, and any supporting materials. An automated acknowledgement with a unique manuscript ID (e.g., IJITRA-2025-XXXX) is sent within 24 hours.
Online submission portal Automated acknowledgement Unique manuscript ID assigned
2
🔍 Editorial Desk Review
Days 1–5
The Managing Editor performs an initial quality check. Manuscripts are screened for: (a) scope alignment with IJITRA, (b) plagiarism using iThenticate (similarity ≤ 15%), (c) format compliance with author guidelines, and (d) completeness of all required sections. Manuscripts failing this check are desk-rejected with an explanation. Those passing proceed to peer review.
iThenticate plagiarism check Scope fit assessment Format compliance
3
👥 Reviewer Invitation & Assignment
Days 5–8
The Section Editor identifies at least two independent domain experts as reviewers, ensuring no conflicts of interest exist. Reviewers are invited and given 10–15 days to complete their review. Authors' names and affiliations are removed before manuscripts are sent to reviewers. If a reviewer declines, alternative reviewers are sought promptly.
Minimum 2 reviewers COI check performed Author identities blinded
4
🔬 Peer Review Evaluation
Days 8–23
Reviewers evaluate the manuscript using a structured form covering: originality, significance, methodological rigour, clarity of presentation, adequacy of references, and appropriateness of conclusions. They provide detailed written comments for the authors and a confidential recommendation for the editor. Late reviewers are sent reminders at days 10, 13, and 15.
Structured review form Confidential editor note Author-facing comments Automated reminders
5
⚖️ Editorial Decision
Days 23–25
Based on reviewer reports, the Section Editor makes a recommendation to the Editor-in-Chief, who issues the final decision. The decision letter includes the full reviewer comments (anonymised). In cases of conflicting reviewer recommendations, a third reviewer may be consulted or the Editor-in-Chief casts the deciding vote.
4 possible decisions Full reviewer comments shared EIC final authority
6
✏️ Revision (if required)
+14 / +30 days
Authors are expected to address all reviewer comments in a point-by-point response letter. Minor revisions must be returned within 14 days; major revisions within 30 days. Revised manuscripts are typically re-reviewed by the same reviewers. Authors may request an extension with valid justification.
Point-by-point response required Tracked changes recommended Same reviewers where possible
7
🎉 Acceptance & Production
+7 days to publish
Upon final acceptance, the manuscript enters production — copyediting, typesetting, author proofing, and galley correction. DOI is registered with CrossRef. The article is published online-first (with full citation details) within 7 days of payment receipt, and included in the next scheduled issue volume.
DOI registered via CrossRef Online-first publication Author proofs sent

Types of Editorial Decisions

✅ Accept ~10%
The manuscript is accepted as submitted with no changes, or only very minor editorial adjustments (spelling, formatting). This is the most favourable outcome and rare on first submission.
  • APC invoice issued within 24 hours
  • Production begins on payment
  • Published online-first within 7 days
🔵 Minor Revision ~20%
The manuscript has merit but requires minor corrections — clarifications, additional references, figure adjustments, or language improvements that don't affect the scientific conclusions.
  • Revise and return within 14 days
  • Point-by-point response required
  • Re-review by Section Editor only
🟡 Major Revision ~42%
Significant concerns need addressing — additional experiments, substantial rewriting, methodological clarifications, or new statistical analysis. The outcome after revision is not guaranteed acceptance.
  • Revise and return within 30 days
  • Full point-by-point response required
  • Re-reviewed by original reviewers
❌ Reject ~28%
The manuscript has fundamental flaws — outside scope, inadequate methodology, fabricated data, insufficient novelty, or irresolvable issues that cannot be fixed through revision.
  • Full reviewer comments provided
  • No APC charged
  • Authors may appeal (see below)

Reviewer Evaluation Criteria

Reviewers assess each manuscript on the following weighted criteria using a structured 1–5 scale. Authors can use this as a checklist when preparing their manuscripts.

CriterionDescriptionWeight
Originality & Novelty Does the paper make a significant new contribution? Is it sufficiently different from prior work?
30%
Scientific Rigour Are the methods sound? Are claims supported by evidence? Is the experimental design appropriate?
25%
Significance & Impact Does the work advance the field? Will it be useful to researchers or practitioners?
20%
Clarity & Presentation Is the paper well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow? Are figures and tables appropriate?
15%
References & Literature Is the literature review comprehensive and up-to-date? Are citations appropriate and complete?
10%

⚖️ How to Appeal a Rejection

Authors who believe a manuscript was rejected unfairly — due to misunderstanding, reviewer bias, or factual errors in the review — may submit a formal appeal within 30 days of the decision. The appeal must include: (1) a point-by-point rebuttal of reviewer concerns, (2) supporting evidence or clarifications, and (3) a statement of why the decision should be reconsidered. Appeals are reviewed by the Editor-in-Chief with a different Associate Editor and decided within 21 days. The Editor-in-Chief's decision on appeals is final.
