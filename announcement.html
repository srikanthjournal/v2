Special Issue on Generative AI in Education: Transforming Learning, Assessment, and Pedagogy — Call for Papers
Generative AIEducationLLMsEdTechAssessment

The International Journal of Innovative Technology, Innovation and Research Applications (IJITRA) invites original research papers for a special thematic issue on Generative AI in Education, scheduled for publication in Volume 3, Issue 3 (July 2025).

About This Special Issue

The rapid emergence of large language models (LLMs) including GPT-4, Gemini, Claude, and open-source alternatives such as LLaMA and Mistral has fundamentally transformed educational practice at all levels. From intelligent tutoring systems and automated essay scoring to AI-generated course content and conversational pedagogical agents, the educational technology landscape is undergoing unprecedented disruption.

This special issue aims to bring together rigorous empirical research, systematic reviews, theoretical frameworks, and case studies that advance our understanding of how generative AI is reshaping teaching, learning, and institutional governance in education.

Topics of Interest

Submissions are invited on, but not limited to, the following topics:

  • Large language models in K-12, higher education, and professional learning contexts
  • AI-assisted and AI-automated assessment: validity, reliability, and fairness
  • Academic integrity in the age of generative AI — detection, policy, and pedagogy
  • Personalised and adaptive learning systems powered by generative AI
  • Conversational AI tutors and intelligent pedagogical agents
  • Student and instructor attitudes, perceptions, and behavioural change
  • AI literacy and digital competence for students and educators
  • Ethical, equity, and access dimensions of AI in education
  • Regulatory and institutional policy frameworks for GenAI in education
  • Multimodal generative AI in STEM, arts, and humanities education
  • Prompt engineering as a pedagogical skill
  • Generative AI for content creation: curriculum design and instructional materials

Guest Editors

  • Dr. Laura Martinez, MIT, Massachusetts, USA — Explainable AI, Educational Data Mining
  • Prof. Sanjay Kapoor, BITS Pilani, India — Neural Networks, AI Applications in Education
  • Dr. Yuki Chen, Tsinghua University, China — Federated Learning, Privacy in EdTech

Important Dates

  • Submission Deadline: May 30, 2025
  • Notification of Decision: June 25, 2025
  • Revised Manuscript Due: July 5, 2025
  • Expected Publication: July 2025 (Vol. 3, Issue 3)

Submission Instructions

Manuscripts must be submitted through the IJITRA online submission system at submit.html. Please select "Special Issue: Generative AI in Education" from the manuscript type dropdown. All submissions must adhere to the standard Author Guidelines.

Manuscripts submitted to this special issue undergo the same rigorous double-blind peer review process as regular issues. Article Processing Charges (APCs) follow standard IJITRA rates — see the APC page for details. Waivers are available.
