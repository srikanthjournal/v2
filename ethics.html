Ethics & Publication Policy

IJITRA is committed to the highest standards of publication ethics. All stakeholders — authors, editors, and reviewers — are bound by these policies.

🏛️ COPE MEMBER

COPE Member Journal

IJITRA is a proud member of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and adheres strictly to its Core Practices and guidelines. All editorial decisions follow COPE's principles of transparency, integrity, and accountability. Authors, editors, and reviewers are expected to comply with COPE guidelines at all times.

1. Authorship Policy

ICMJE Criteria

Authorship of a manuscript submitted to IJITRA must be based on the ICMJE criteria. All listed authors must have:

  • Made substantial contributions to the conception or design of the work, or the acquisition, analysis, or interpretation of data;
  • Drafted the work or reviewed it critically for important intellectual content;
  • Approved the final version to be published;
  • Agreed to be accountable for all aspects of the work, ensuring that questions related to the accuracy or integrity of any part of the work are appropriately investigated and resolved.

Individuals who contributed to the work but do not meet all four authorship criteria should be listed in the Acknowledgements section instead.

Ghost authorship (undisclosed paid authorship), guest authorship (listing prominent individuals who made no contribution), and gift authorship are forms of misconduct that will result in immediate rejection or retraction.

All manuscripts must include a CRediT (Contributor Roles Taxonomy) statement clearly attributing each author's specific contribution (e.g., Conceptualization, Methodology, Software, Validation, Formal analysis, Investigation, Resources, Writing – original draft, Writing – review & editing, Visualization, Supervision, Project administration, Funding acquisition).

2. Peer Review Policy

Double-Blind

IJITRA operates a rigorous double-blind peer review process, where both authors and reviewers remain anonymous to each other throughout the review. All submitted manuscripts undergo the following process:

Step 1 — Initial SubmissionAuthors submit via the online portal. The manuscript receives an ID within 24 hours.
Step 2 — Editorial Desk Review (3–5 days)The Managing Editor checks for scope fit, format compliance, and plagiarism. Manuscripts outside scope or above 15% similarity are desk-rejected.
Step 3 — Reviewer Assignment (2–3 days)The Section Editor assigns at least two independent expert reviewers. Reviewers are selected based on domain expertise and absence of conflicts of interest.
Step 4 — Peer Review (10–15 days)Reviewers evaluate the manuscript for scientific rigor, originality, clarity, and significance. They provide a detailed report and one of four recommendations.
Step 5 — Editorial DecisionBased on reviewer reports, the Editor-in-Chief decides: Accept, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Reject. Authors receive full reviewer comments.
Step 6 — Revision & Re-review (if required)Authors have 14 days (minor revision) or 30 days (major revision) to respond. Revised manuscripts are re-evaluated by the same reviewers where possible.
Step 7 — Final Decision & ProductionAccepted manuscripts enter copyediting, typesetting, and proofing before final publication with DOI assignment.

Reviewers are expected to maintain confidentiality, declare conflicts of interest, provide constructive and objective assessments, and avoid using unpublished information from reviewed manuscripts for personal advantage.

3. Plagiarism & Originality Policy

iThenticate Checked

IJITRA uses iThenticate (Turnitin's plagiarism detection software) to screen all submitted manuscripts before peer review. Submissions are also checked for self-plagiarism and duplicate publication.

Similarity LevelAction TakenSeverity
≤ 15% overall (no single source > 5%)Manuscript proceeds to peer reviewAcceptable
15%–25% overallAuthors notified; manuscript returned for revision with explanationCaution
> 25% overallImmediate rejection without review; authors notifiedRejected
Verbatim copying (> 5% from single source)Rejection regardless of overall similarityRejected
Fabricated or manipulated data detectedRejection + institutional notificationMisconduct

Self-plagiarism: Authors may not submit manuscripts that substantially overlap with their previously published work without proper disclosure and permission. Overlapping material of more than 20% with a prior publication requires a signed explanation and must be properly cited.

Duplicate Submission: Submitting the same manuscript to multiple journals simultaneously is strictly prohibited. Authors must confirm the manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere.

4. Conflicts of Interest

Mandatory Disclosure

A conflict of interest (COI) exists when an author, reviewer, or editor has financial, personal, professional, or institutional interests that could inappropriately influence their objectivity. All potential COIs must be disclosed at the time of submission.

Authors must disclose: funding sources, employment, stock ownership, honoraria, consulting fees, expert testimony, patents (pending or received), royalties, or any other financial relationship relevant to the submitted work.

Reviewers must decline to review a manuscript if they have: collaborated with any author in the past 3 years, been a direct supervisor or student of any author, a financial interest in the outcome, or any personal relationship that could bias their judgment.

Editors must recuse themselves from handling manuscripts submitted by colleagues, former students, collaborators, or anyone with whom they have a personal conflict.

If no conflicts exist, authors must include the statement: "The authors declare no conflict of interest."

5. Corrections, Retractions & Expressions of Concern

COPE Guidelines

IJITRA follows COPE guidelines for handling post-publication corrections. Published articles may be corrected, retracted, or accompanied by an Expression of Concern under the following circumstances:

  • Correction (Erratum/Corrigendum): Issued when a minor error is discovered that does not affect the scientific conclusions. The original article is updated with a visible correction notice and version history.
  • Retraction: Issued when a published article contains major errors, fabricated/falsified data, plagiarism, duplicate publication, or unethical research. The retraction notice names the reason and remains linked to the original article permanently.
  • Expression of Concern: Issued when the editorial office has evidence of possible misconduct but investigations are ongoing, or when a response from an institution cannot be obtained.
  • Removal: In rare legal cases (e.g., defamatory content), an article may be removed with a notice explaining the reason.

All corrections and retractions are published as citable items, linked to the original article via CrossRef, and follow the COPE retraction guidelines. IJITRA does not remove or alter published articles without a formal notice.

6. Human Subjects & Animal Research Ethics

Declaration of Helsinki

Research involving human participants must have been conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and must have received approval from an appropriate Institutional Review Board (IRB) or Ethics Committee. The manuscript must state the name of the approving body and the approval number.

Informed consent must have been obtained from all human participants. Where identifiable information is presented (photographs, case details), written consent must be confirmed.

Research involving animals must comply with applicable national and institutional guidelines for the care and use of laboratory animals. The manuscript must state approval from the relevant Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) or equivalent body.

Manuscripts that do not provide adequate evidence of ethical approval will be rejected without review.

📊

7. Data Sharing & Reproducibility

Open Data Encouraged

IJITRA strongly encourages authors to make research data, code, and materials openly available to support transparency and reproducibility. Authors are required to include a Data Availability Statement in all manuscripts, indicating whether data is:

  • Publicly available (with URL/repository link and access date)
  • Available on reasonable request from the corresponding author
  • Not sharable due to ethical, legal, or proprietary restrictions (with explanation)

Recommended data repositories include: Zenodo, Figshare, OSF, GitHub, IEEE DataPort, and domain-specific repositories. For AI/ML papers, we encourage sharing model weights, training code, and benchmark results.

Editors and reviewers may request access to underlying data during the review process if reproducibility concerns arise.

Stakeholder Responsibilities at a Glance

📝 Authors
  • Submit only original, unpublished work
  • Accurately represent data and findings
  • List all qualifying authors only
  • Disclose all conflicts of interest
  • Obtain required ethical approvals
  • Correct errors promptly if discovered
  • Respond to reviewer comments fairly
🎓 Editors
  • Evaluate manuscripts based on merit alone
  • Maintain reviewer confidentiality
  • Recuse from COI situations
  • Process manuscripts in timely fashion
  • Investigate allegations of misconduct
  • Implement corrections and retractions
  • Preserve editorial independence
🔍 Reviewers
  • Review only manuscripts in their expertise
  • Maintain strict confidentiality
  • Declare conflicts of interest
  • Provide objective, constructive feedback
  • Complete reviews on time
  • Not use unpublished ideas without consent
  • Alert editors to suspected misconduct
